EGR 106 - Foundations of Engineering II                 

 

Course Description: Engineering problem solving. (Lec. 1, Lab. 2) Prerequisities: MTH 141 or concurrent registration in MTH 141.

Course Goals: To develop engineering problem solving skills using the computer software tool MATLAB - a widely used engineering and scientific software package with excellent computational, programming and graphics features. Students will acquire skills to write basic MATLAB codes to solve problems of engineering interest. These skills will be applied to an engineering design project.

General Education Outcome: By completing the EGR 105/106 course sequence, students will have satisfied the "Arts and Design" outcome. The Accreditation Board for Engineering and Techology (ABET) defines Engineering Design as the process of devising a system, component, or process to meet desired needs. It is a decision-making process (often iterative), in which the basic sciences, mathematics, and the engineering sciences are applied to convert resources optimally to meet these stated needs. In EGR 105, students will be introduced to and reflect on aspects of design as it relates to each engineering discipline represented at URI. In both EGR 105 and 106, students will seek to learn engineering design through practice in team projects culminating with a presentation to peers and the course instructor. EGR 106 will also satisfy partial coverage of the "Mathematical, Statistical, or Computational Strategies" outcome. Mathematical and computational concepts are introduced and/or reinforced through programming topics such as array mathematics, 2-D plotting, loops, user-defined functions, and conditional statements. Statistical concepts will be reinforced through Matlab functions for computing statistical parameters such as mean and standard deviation and plotting of histograms. The design project will apply concepts of analytic geometry in defining 3-D geometric shapes such as spheres, cylinders and hexahedra.

Software: Matlab (free download for URI Students).

Text: Matlab - An Introduction with Applications, 6th edition, A. Gilat, John Wiley & Sons, 2015, Free online access: click here, Under institution, select "Not Listed, click here" and enter URI login credentials.

Course Operation: This course is composed of 3 general sections that nominally meet on Monday (M 4-4:50 PM, 5-5:50 PM and 6-6:50 PM) and 13 smaller sections that meet on Tuesday or Thursday (see table below). Each student should be registered for one Monday section and one Tuesday/Thursday section. On Monday, January 24, a live synchronous Zoom meeting will be held at 4 PM and a video lecture will be posted for those who are unable to attend the 4 PM Zoom meeting. Starting on Monday, January 31, an in-person lecture will be held in Pastore Auditorium (Room 124) at 4 PM and a video lecture will be posted for those who are unable to attend the 4 PM in-person lecture. Note that all students in all Monday sections are welcome to attend the 4 PM lecture or, if they prefer, view the recorded video lectures on their own time. Note: T-Th sections WILL MEET on Tuesday, Jan. 25 and Thursday, Jan 27.

The Monday lectures will provide theory and background needed in preparation for the weekly problems assigned on Tuesday/Thursday. Lectures will posted on Brightspace and should be reviewed prior to attending Tuesday/Thursday classes. Starting the week of January 31, all students must take a weekly online quiz before 8 AM on Tuesday mornings. Tuesday-Thursday class meetings will provide time, setting and help for completion of weekly assignments. All students must take a common exam (Exam 1) on February 28 and an exam specific to each T-Th section (Exam 2) during the week of March 28. Weekly assignments will be distributed by each T-Th instructor and may vary from section to section.

A team design project will be introduced early in the semester and weekly assignment problems will build programming skills required for project completion. Weeks 10-13 will be devoted to developing Matlab scripts used for your design. Each team will prepare a project proposal including a description of the component to be designed, a project schedule and roles of each of the team members. During finals week, each team will present their design to their T-Th instructor and classmates.

Exams: This class will include two semester exams. Exam 1 will be common to all sections and will be given on February 28. Exam 2 is scheduled during the March 29 and April 1 class periods and will involve writing functioning MATLAB code. Further details on these exams will be given. Contact your T-Th section instructor immediately if you are not able to take an exam on the scheduled date.

Weekly Assignments: Assignments will be distributed within each T-Th class. In some cases, weekly assignments will be completed during the T-Th session, but some assignments may require additional outside work. Students should follow instructions of their T-Th instructor for submission procedures, due dates, etc.

Grading: Weekly quizzes - 10%, Weekly assignments - 25%, Semester Exams - 35%, Project - 20%, T-Th class attendance - 10%. The Tuesday-Thursday instructors will compile final course grades based on these components and assign letter grades.

Academic Integrity: According to URI's policy on academic integrity (see pgs. 12-13), "A student’s name on any written work including assignments, lab reports, internship reports, papers, or examinations, shall be regarded as assurance that the work is the result of the student’s own thought and study." While group study is generally encouraged in this course, each student is expected to submit work that they have developed on their own. Submission of copied MATLAB code is not acceptable. Similarly, while collaboration in preparing for quizzes is acceptable, online quizzes and exams are to be taken independently.

Disability Accommodations: Students with a documented disability are welcome to request accommodations and should contact their instructor as soon as possible. For more information, contact Disability Services for Students Office, 330 Memorial Union, 874-2098, www.uri.edu/disability_services
